Central Bank Policy Curves

Forward-looking rate expectations by currency. See which central banks are hawkish (tightening/hiking) vs dovish (easing/cutting). Spot macro trend shifts forming in FX before they happen.

▶ ℹ️ How to use: Central Bank Policy
1
Read the cards: Each central bank shows current rate, stance (hawkish/dovish/neutral), next meeting, and last action.
2
Rate curve chart: Solid line = historical rates. Dashed line = market forward expectations. Divergence = opportunity.
3
The edge: Hawkish = currency strengthens. Dovish = currency weakens. Policy divergence between two banks = the strongest FX trends.
4
Insights box: Bottom section shows the system's macro FX conclusions — e.g. "Sell EUR/GBP — weakest vs strongest policy divergence."
💡 This is the most institutional-grade tool in the dashboard. Hedge funds pay millions for this data. Use it alongside FX Strength for maximum conviction.

Buy Below the Mean

The mean is the average of ALL historical closing prices. When the current price is below this mean, you're buying at a discount to the long-term fair value — a good time to DCA in. The further below, the better the value.

▶ ℹ️ How to use: Buy Below Mean
1
Select an ETF: Choose from the dropdown to see its cumulative historical mean.
2
Read the chart: Blue line = price. Yellow line = cumulative mean of ALL historical closes. Green shading = price below mean.
3
The signal: When price is below the yellow mean line, you're buying at a statistical discount to long-term fair value.
4
Stats: Shows % of time price has been below mean, average entry price when buying only below mean, and the DCA discount.
💡 This is a different lens from the discount tracker. Instead of measuring from ATH, it measures from the mathematical average. Both below mean AND below Tier 1 = strongest buy signal.

Currency Strength

Relative strength of major currencies based on today's moves across key FX pairs. Stronger currencies appear higher.

▶ ℹ️ How to use: FX Currency Strength
1
Read the rankings: Currencies ranked from strongest (top) to weakest (bottom). Bar length shows relative strength.
2
The strategy: Buy the strongest currency against the weakest. If GBP is +0.8 and NZD is −0.6, consider buying GBP/NZD.
3
How it works: Calculates daily % change across 12 major FX pairs, then averages each currency's performance.
4
Combine with CB Policy: If GBP is strongest AND the BOE is hawkish, that's a high-conviction long.
💡 Strongest vs weakest pairs tend to trend. Use this to find the best FX pair to trade each day.
Click Load FX Data
Currency strength is calculated by averaging the daily % change of each currency across all its major pairs (e.g. USD strength = average of EURUSD⁻¹, GBPUSD⁻¹, USDJPY, USDCHF, AUDUSD⁻¹, USDCAD, NZDUSD⁻¹). A positive score means the currency gained today. Useful for spotting which currencies to buy and sell in FX trading.
