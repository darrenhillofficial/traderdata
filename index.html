How to Trade Contrarian Sentiment:

● 75%+ Retail Long → They've bought the top. Smart money will push price down to trigger stops. Look for shorts.
● 75%+ Retail Short → They've sold the bottom. Smart money will squeeze them up. Look for longs.
● 50-65% → No clear edge. Wait for extremes.

Combine with other tabs:
• CB Policy: If retail is 75% long on a currency whose central bank just turned dovish → very strong short
• Comm→FX: If retail is 75% short on CAD and oil is surging → strong long (fundamental + contrarian)
• FX Strength: Sell the weakest currency where retail is most long, buy the strongest where retail is most short

Buy Below the Mean

The mean is the average of ALL historical closing prices. When the current price is below this mean, you're buying at a discount to the long-term fair value — a good time to DCA in. The further below, the better the value.

Currency Strength

Relative strength of major currencies based on today's moves across key FX pairs. Stronger currencies appear higher.

Click Load FX Data
Currency strength is calculated by averaging the daily % change of each currency across all its major pairs (e.g. USD strength = average of EURUSD⁻¹, GBPUSD⁻¹, USDJPY, USDCHF, AUDUSD⁻¹, USDCAD, NZDUSD⁻¹). A positive score means the currency gained today. Useful for spotting which currencies to buy and sell in FX trading.
